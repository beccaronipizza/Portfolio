About Me

I’m a wetland scientist with a passion for data visualization, ecological restoration, and science communication. My experience includes field monitoring, GIS analysis, and public engagement. I'm driven to support climate resilience and community-focused conservation efforts.

Thesis Project

My graduate thesis evaluated the effectiveness of nature-based shoreline stabilization techniques used to reduce lateral marsh edge erosion at the Sears Point Levee Adaptive Management Project in San Pablo Bay. Over the first three years post-restoration, I conducted monthly vegetation surveys and elevation monitoring to assess ecological and geomorphic responses. I presented my findings at CERF, the State of the Estuary Conference, and SERCAL.
