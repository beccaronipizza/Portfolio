Thesis Project

My graduate thesis focused on the effectiveness of nature-based shoreline stabilization techniques in San Pablo Bay. I conducted monthly vegetation surveys and elevation monitoring, and presented my findings at CERF, State of the Estuary, and SERCAL.

Outreach

I led community science field trips, supported wetland delineation certification courses, and assisted in public engagement at the San Francisco Bay NERR.

Vegetation Surveys

I conducted detailed plant surveys in tidal marshes using quadrats and transects, supporting long-term ecological monitoring projects in brackish and saline marshes.

Eelgrass Monitoring & Restoration

I helped monitor restoration sites, process invertebrate and epiphyte samples, and assisted in field deployment of oyster-enclosed crab traps in challenging field conditions.

GIS & Mapping

Using ArcGIS Pro, I created maps of restoration sites, delineated riparian areas, and analyzed LiDAR and aerial imagery to support environmental assessments and planning.

Elevation Monitoring

I used SET wells, Emlid RTK GPS, Trimble RTK, and Total Station surveys to monitor marsh elevation changes over time, contributing to climate resilience studies.

Water Quality Monitoring

Deployed and calibrated YSI sondes in the Bay and mesocosms, retrieved and managed data, and assessed trends in salinity, temperature, DO, and more.

Data Analysis & Visualization

I used R to clean and analyze field data, producing publication-quality plots and figures for ecological monitoring reports and my thesis presentation.
